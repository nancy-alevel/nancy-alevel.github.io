Background

Climate change, the shortage of cheap clean energy and water resources not only threaten the survival of human civilization, but also threaten the sustainable social development and prosperity of the world. Nuclear energy is considered to be the future.

The aim of this project is to explore new research and development in the field of nuclear fusion energy and design an innovative Magetic-Inertial Confinement Fusion (MIF) Energy System.

Coaxial plasma gun is the key to this project and has been researched and developed for more than 70 years for a variety of applications, notably as a plasma thruster for space propulsion, creating a large and extensive physics and engineering knowledge base.

As a research engineer (mechanical), I am responsible for designing and optimizing the plasma gun, conducting pratical experiments to test the feasibility and working performance of the gun, developing the vacuum chamber system of each experimental platform and carrying out computational simulation of the physics process and structurak analysis using USim and ANSYS. I am also involved in other physics experiments: Spark-gap switch, Argon Preionization, High energy density capacitors, etc.

Remarkable Milestones

Plasma Gun Design (Patent Application in progress)

The plasma gun, also known as an electromagnetic launcher, consists of three main sections: Gun electrode, Gas Valve and Main Capacitor Bank at the rear end. I am responsible for designing the Gas Valve and the Gun electrodes.

The Gas Valve is the key part of the gun. Its working principle is the use of an electromagnetic coil to drive the flyer plate up and down to realize opening and closing functions; in the meantime, a small amout of Argon gas is dispensed into the electrodes, followed by a pre-ionization, and then launched in the form of a plasma jet.

Under the supervision of Prof. Thio, I developed an all-new gas valve design for STG1. The original reciprocating valve is updated to an innovative unidirectional valve. This new design reduces the valve's opening time from 300μs down to 150-200μs based on simulation results and precise Laser Displacement Sensor (by KEYENCE) testing results.

The first generation plasma gun was designed based on careful production engineering analysis.

Vacuum Chamber System Design

Apart from the plasma gun development, I also designed and set up TWO vacuum chamber systems: one for Gun Development Platform; the other for Gas Valve & Pre-ionization Platform. They are now both in service. They provide an ultimate vacuum up to 10^(-7) torr.

The two complete vacuum systems were all designed and developed independently by myself, including the main vacuum tank, quartz window, flanges, vacuum gauges (ionization gauge & Pirani gauge), feedthrough, vacuum pumps, digital control console, cooling water system, gas supply system, etc.

MHD (Magnetohydrodynamics) Modeling

To verify the analytical results, we conducted 2D MHD modeling using the code in USIM. The 2D MHD modeling results and the analytical results have excellent agreement.

USIM used in two-dimensional simulation is a computational fluid dynamics simulation program for supersonic flow and magnetohydrodynamics. It uses the finite volume method to solve the plasma flow problem.
